We are a house of wood and shadow: paintings, objects and small stories arranged like breath. Take the long way. Let the light decide what you see first.

Admit One

Rooms

Our rooms are conversations—each with its own temperature: maritime, portrait, instrument, paper. Step across thresholds, not floors.

The Portrait Room

Lenses: empathy, posture, eye-line

Faces that hold the room together. Labels are small; silence is part of the work.

The Maritime

Lenses: weather, distance, time

Maps and salt. Ropes like handwriting. A tide between frames.

Paper & Light

Lenses: margin, breath, tactility

Drawings you can hear. Margins wider than a sentence.

Current Exhibition — “Lines that Remember”

Works on paper and wood from unhurried hands. Rubbings, notations, fragments of rooms. Nothing is precious; everything is careful.

The House

The Meridian was once a carpenter’s workshop. We kept the walls. The frames know they’re made of older trees. We hang with patience and change with the weather.

Please: look slowly.

Workshop Wall

We owe our straight lines to the bench that used to live here.
